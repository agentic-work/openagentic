# FedRAMP Authorization for AI/ML Systems: 2025–2026

## Executive Summary

Federal agencies are accelerating AI/ML adoption under **Executive Order 14110** (Safe, Secure, and Trustworthy AI) and **OMB M-24-10** (Advancing Governance, Innovation, and Risk Management for Agency Use of AI). Cloud Service Providers (CSPs) seeking FedRAMP authorization for AI/ML workloads face a **converging regulatory landscape** where traditional FedRAMP controls merge with the **NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI 100-1)** and emerging requirements from the **FedRAMP 20x modernization initiative**. There is no standalone "FedRAMP for AI" — instead, AI/ML systems must satisfy the existing **FedRAMP Moderate baseline (~325 controls from NIST SP 800-53 Rev 5)** plus additional AI-specific risk management expectations.

### 4. Fact Check: AI/ML Model Drift Monitoring

> **Claim:** "FedRAMP requires continuous monitoring for AI/ML model drift"

**Verdict: Partially True — Emerging Requirement**

- **FedRAMP ConMon (CA-7)** mandates continuous monitoring of the security posture, but the traditional program focuses on vulnerability scanning, configuration management, and incident response — not model performance drift.
- **NIST AI RMF MEASURE 2.6** explicitly addresses monitoring for "concept drift, data drift, and model degradation."
- **OMB M-24-10** requires agencies to implement "ongoing monitoring" of AI systems including performance and impact.
- As of early 2025, there is **no explicit FedRAMP control requiring model drift monitoring**, but the convergence of AI RMF + OMB guidance + ConMon requirements creates a **de facto expectation** that CSPs offering AI/ML services will monitor for drift as part of their continuous monitoring program.
- **Confidence:** ⚠️ Medium — trajectory is clear, explicit FedRAMP control text pending.
